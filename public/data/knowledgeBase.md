# AML Investigations — Standard Operating Procedures

## Overview
This knowledge base documents the standard operating procedures for Anti-Money Laundering (AML) investigations at Meridian Bank. It covers the end-to-end workflow from alert triage through final disposition, including escalation protocols, regulatory filing requirements, and documentation standards.

## Investigation Workflow

### 1. Alert Triage & Assignment
- Alerts generated by transaction monitoring system are assigned based on priority level
- L1 alerts: Routine review by junior analysts
- L2 alerts: Complex cases requiring senior investigator review
- Priority scoring based on: transaction amount, counterparty risk, geographic risk, behavioral patterns

### 2. Data Collection & KYC Review
- Pull customer profile from core banking system
- Review KYC file: identification documents, beneficial ownership, source of funds
- Extract transaction history (minimum 24 months)
- Identify all linked accounts and related parties

### 3. Transaction Analysis
- Map transaction flows using network visualization
- Identify structuring patterns (deposits just below reporting thresholds)
- Flag round-dollar transactions, rapid movement of funds, layering patterns
- Cross-reference with known typologies: trade-based ML, funnel accounts, shell company networks

### 4. Enhanced Due Diligence
- Adverse media screening via LexisNexis, World-Check, Google News
- Corporate registry searches (state SOS, SEC EDGAR, international registries)
- Beneficial ownership verification through OpenCorporates, corporate filings
- Social media and web presence assessment
- Sanctions list screening: OFAC SDN, EU Consolidated, UN Security Council

### 5. Risk Assessment & Scoring
- Dynamic risk scoring updated throughout investigation
- Factors: transaction volume, geographic risk, entity complexity, adverse media findings
- Score ranges: 0-40 (Low), 41-60 (Medium), 61-80 (High), 81-100 (Critical)
- Automatic escalation triggers at score thresholds

### 6. Request for Information (RFI)
- Standardized RFI templates for customer outreach
- Response deadline: 10 business days
- Escalation if no response: second notice at 15 days, account restriction at 30 days
- All RFI correspondence logged in case file

### 7. SAR Filing Decision
- Investigation narrative must document: suspicious activity description, subject information, financial institution information
- SAR required when: investigation confirms suspicious activity that cannot be explained by legitimate business purpose
- Filing deadline: 30 calendar days from initial detection (60 days if subject not identified)
- FinCEN BSA E-Filing system for electronic submission

### 8. Case Disposition
- **Cleared**: No suspicious activity found. Document rationale. Close case.
- **SAR Filed**: Suspicious activity confirmed. File SAR, implement enhanced monitoring.
- **Escalated**: Complex cases requiring MLRO or law enforcement referral.
- All dispositions require supervisor approval before finalization.

## Regulatory Framework
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) / Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- USA PATRIOT Act Section 314(a) and 314(b)
- FinCEN SAR filing requirements (31 CFR 1020.320)
- OFAC compliance and sanctions screening
- Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC) BSA/AML Manual

## Documentation Standards
- All investigation steps must be timestamped and attributed
- Evidence artifacts: transaction records, KYC documents, screening results, correspondence
- Case summary required for every closed investigation
- Retention period: minimum 5 years from case closure date
